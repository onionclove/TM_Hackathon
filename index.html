1. Introduction

Context, executive summary, objective, scope, assumptions.

Context

We performed threat modelling on an online browser-based multiplayer city-building game and narrowed the system into manageable components for deeper analysis.

Executive Summary

We focused on three high-impact areas: authentication, media uploads, and payment/marketplace. Key risks center around financial loss, abuse of external callbacks, and integrity of user assets and transactions.

Objective

The objective of this threat modelling exercise is to identify risks in the game’s system and elaborate on their business impact, followed by development of recommendations on how these risks can be mitigated to minimise said impact. We have prioritised the following goals to achieve using our threat model:

  • User safety
  • Game integrity/Fair play
  • Financial integrity

Scope & Assumptions

  • In scope: internal components modeled in our DFDs (focus areas below).
  • Out of scope: external entities and systems not explicitly modeled (except the listed external payment gateway).
  • Assumptions: Certain protocol and control details are assumed where architecture isn't fully specified.

Business Consideration

This threat modeling exercise directly supports business continuity and risk management objectives. By identifying and mitigating threats to our authentication, media upload, and payment systems, we:

  • Protect revenue streams: Prevent payment fraud, unauthorized refunds, and marketplace manipulation that could result in direct financial losses.
  • Maintain user trust: Safeguard user credentials and personal data to preserve our reputation and avoid costly breaches that could lead to user churn.
  • Ensure platform integrity: Prevent malicious content uploads and moderation system abuse that could expose users to harmful content and regulatory penalties.
  • Reduce operational costs: Minimize the impact of denial-of-service attacks and system abuse that could overwhelm infrastructure and increase operational expenses.
  • Meet compliance requirements: Add security features to align with major regulations such as GDPR, focusing on user safety and privacy.
